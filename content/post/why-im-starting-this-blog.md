I've been building, breaking, fixing, and optimizing systems for years. Sometimes I do it for work, often for fun, and almost always with secondhand gear and buckets of curiosity. Lately, I’ve felt a growing urge to document and share the journey. Not because I have everything figured out, but because writing helps me think.

This blog is my public notebook. It’s a space for reflection, exploration, and maybe even a bit of teaching. Here’s why I’m starting it.

---

## **Debugging my Thoughts**

Some of my clearest ideas have come out of messy notes or long-form rambling in Teams. Writing helps me organize my thoughts and spot gaps in my reasoning. When I write things down, I understand them more deeply. When I share them, I invite new perspectives. This blog is where I’ll work through ideas, test assumptions, and hopefully grow from the process.

---

## **Building Cool Things with Old Gear**

I’ve built some pretty cool things using secondhand equipment. My reverse proxy setup, a media server with GPU acceleration, and DNS-level filtering using Pi-hole are just a few examples. This blog is a place to dive into the details, explain how I got them working, and show off what comes from doing a lot with a little.

---

## **Get Better at Technical Writing**

Writing clearly about technical topics is a valuable skill, and I want to improve my ability to explain how things work and why I built them the way I did. Practicing through blog posts is a great way to build that muscle and share ideas that might help others. I’ve always felt a bit self-conscious about my writing style, but with today’s tools offering feedback and suggestions, the process feels less daunting and more empowering. This blog will also serve as a journal of my technical adventures.

---

## **Travel Blog for My Journey Through My Tech Career**

When I go on a trip, I like to make sketches of places and people I see along the way. It reminds me of things I experienced and people I have met. 
I’ve lost count of how many times I’ve solved a problem in a clever way and then completely forgotten how I did it. This blog is where I’ll record those moments. It’s a way to capture wins, track learning, and keep a log of the ideas and projects that might otherwise get lost.

---

## **Disaster Recovery for My Brain**

Hardware fails. Software breaks. Sometimes I revisit a setup and can’t remember what I was thinking or how I configured something. By documenting my process and decisions, I’m building a recovery plan for myself. This blog will serve as my own personal reference, especially during troubleshooting or rebuilds.

---

## **What to Expect**

If you’re into self-hosting, home labs, Docker, reverse proxies, Raspberry Pis, and pushing old enterprise hardware to its limits, this blog might be right up your alley. I’ll share how I build things, what works, what doesn’t, and everything I learn along the way.

Thanks for stopping by. Let’s build cool things.
